Sprout Group's Biodegradable Solution Accepted for The SUP Challenge - Goa

Sprout Group's innovative solution to the problem of single-use plastic waste has been selected for The SUP Challenge - Goa. The challenge, organized by The Incubation Network and funded by the German Federal Ministry for Economic Cooperation and Development (BMZ) and ECCA Family Foundation, aims to accelerate startups in South and Southeast Asia to pilot their solutions with the F&B industry to reduce single-use plastic waste. Sprout Group's biodegradable alternative to the SUP coating has been developed using agricultural waste, including cellulose-rich fruit and vegetable waste, and cornstarch. Their solution caught the attention of Climate Collective, who found it to be noteworthy and recommended it for the SUP Challenge. As part of the challenge, eight Entrepreneur Support Organizations (ESOs) will receive at least US$50,000 in funding and technical assistance to support their projects from April until September 2022. The cohort will also contribute their experience and best practices towards a Technical Assistance Playbook for ESOs developed by The Incubation Network. The results of the pilots will contribute to a study around understanding the environmental impacts of alternatives to single-use plastic packaging. This will outline the environmental considerations associated with single-use replacement business models and the opportunities to reduce negative impacts or recognize carbon savings, relative to virgin plastic production. The SUP Challenge - Goa presents a fantastic opportunity for Sprout Group to showcase their innovative solution and make a significant impact on reducing single use plastic waste. We wish them all the best for their participation in the challenge and look forward to seeing the results of their pilot project.
