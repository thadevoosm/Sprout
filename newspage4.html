KERALA VYAPARI VYAVASAYI EKOPANA SAMITHI URGES RETAILERS TO USE BIODEGRADABLE PAPER CUPS IN WAKE OF PLASTIC BAN

The Kerala Vyapari Vyavasayi Ekopana Samithi has released a press notice urging all retailers and distributors in Kerala to use Sprout Greenpak paper cups, which are biodegradable, home compostable, and recyclable. The notice comes in the wake of a ban on single-use plastic products in Kerala since January 2020.

The association strictly prohibits the sale of any banned single-use paper products and will not offer any assistance to those caught selling them. The association is promoting the use of only eco-friendly products and is asking all sellers in Kerala to sell only biodegradable paper cups.

The government of Kerala has banned the use of plastic-coated paper cups as they are harmful to health and the environment. The ban has prompted the Kerala Vyapari Vyavasayi Ekopana Samithi to urge all traders in the state to switch to eco-friendly products.

"Kerala has always been at the forefront of promoting eco-friendly products and practices. The ban on plastic-coated paper cups is a step in the right direction towards a cleaner and healthier environment," said the association during the press meet.

Sprout Greenpak paper cups are approved by the government of Kerala and are made from biodegradable materials. They are also home compostable, which means they can be disposed of safely at home. The cups are also recyclable, making them a perfect alternative to plastic-coated paper cups.

The Kerala Vyapari Vyavasayi Ekopana Samithi's call to use only eco-friendly products is a step in the right direction towards a cleaner and greener Kerala. The association's strict enforcement of the ban on banned single-use paper products is commendable and should be supported by all stakeholders in the state
