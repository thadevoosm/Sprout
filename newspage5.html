SPROUT GROUP EXHIBITS SUSTAINABLE ALTERNATIVES AT GLOBAL EXPO ON WASTE MANAGEMENT TECHNOLOGIES

Sprout Group, a socially responsible organization committed to finding sustainable solutions for environmental challenges, recently participated in the Global Expo on Waste Management Technologies. The event, held on 4th, 5th, and 6th February 2023 at Marine Drive, Kochi, was organized by Suchitwa Mission for the Local Self Government Department, Government of Kerala, and it provided an incredible opportunity for Sprout Group to showcase their alternative products for single-use plastics.

During the event, Sprout Group's team connected with experts, industry leaders, and policymakers and showcased their innovative products, which have the potential to revolutionize the way we consume and dispose of single-use plastics. The company's commitment to the protection and enhancement of the environment was evident through their high environmental performance, achieved by their actions in line with their policy, which sits alongside their Sustainability policy as part of their global policy framework, underpinned by their Global Code of Conduct.

"We are thrilled to have been a part of this important event and grateful to Suchitwa Mission for giving us this opportunity to present our ideas and solutions," said a representative from Sprout Group. "The event was a huge success, and it would not have been possible without the tireless efforts of the organizers and volunteers who made it happen. We are proud to have been a part of this important initiative, and we will continue to work towards creating a better, cleaner, and more sustainable world."

Sprout Group's participation in the Global Expo on Waste Management Technologies is a testament to their commitment to finding sustainable solutions for environmental challenges. The event was a platform for industry players to showcase their products and ideas, and Sprout Group's participation highlighted the importance of finding innovative and ecofriendly alternatives to single-use plastics. Their participation was well received and demonstrated their dedication to creating a cleaner and more sustainable future
